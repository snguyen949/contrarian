Volume at Capitulation — Anna Coulling's Smart Money Footprint

Per A Complete Guide to Volume Price Analysis: institutions accumulate at the bottom during retail panic. Record volume at the lows = smart money absorbing supply while headlines scream doom. Ultra-high volume on down bars followed by narrowing range = accumulation. No extreme volume now = no institutional capitulation bottom being formed yet.

Coulling's Rule: When volume is 2–3x average on a down bar with narrowing spread, smart money is absorbing. When volume is low on a down bar, there's no institutional demand yet — more downside likely.
Headlines at the Bottom vs Now — The Contrarian Signal

Institutions use media to install fear at the lows (to buy cheap from panicked retail) and euphoria at the highs (to distribute to greedy retail). When every headline screams "end of the world" — that's the buy signal. When headlines say "new era" — that's the sell signal. Compare the headline severity below.
